The Problem

Snakebite envenoming is a life-threatening emergency. Globally there are ~5.4 million snakebites per year, causing 63,400+ deaths and ~400,000 permanent disabilities. In many remote regions, victims cannot obtain antivenom within the critical golden 3-hour emergency window due to slow, infrastructure-dependent transport.

63,400+ deaths / year
400,000 disabilities / year

Why urgency is rising

  • Rural population aging: more vulnerable patients in rural areas
  • Climate change: snake habitats expanding into previously safer zones
  • Healthcare worker shortages: limited ground-response capacity in remote areas

Our goal is to make “timely antivenom access” reliable — even when roads are not.

Our Solution

VitalWing is a fixed-wing tailsitter VTOL UAV for emergency antivenom delivery. It takes off and lands vertically in confined areas, then transitions into efficient fixed-wing cruise for long-range, high-speed flight — while maintaining 2–8°C using passive insulation.

What makes it effective

  • Speed + range: fixed-wing lift enables efficient 70 km/h cruise and ~60 km service radius
  • Runway-free ops: VTOL enables clinics and mountainous landing zones
  • Cold-chain stability: optimized PP-board insulation keeps 2–8°C for 80+ minutes
  • Cost: single-trip cost below ¥50 supports sustainable operations

Compared to ambulances (often 3+ hours) and multicopters (<30 km radius), VitalWing targets rapid, reliable last-mile delivery.

Innovation

Novel transition control (the hard part)

Unlike conventional drones that rely mainly on accelerometer data, VitalWing integrates real-time airspeed sensing with pitch-angle feedback through a custom-tuned PID controller. This dual-parameter approach enables reliable VTOL ↔ cruise transitions even under turbulent mountain wind conditions.

95% success transition reliability
70–80% typical industry baseline

Cold-chain without heavy refrigeration

An optimized PP-board insulation payload bay maintains antivenom at 2–8°C for 80+ minutes, improving reliability while keeping the system lightweight and power-efficient.

Defensibility

Go-to-Market

Customer segmentation

  • Primary customer (decision maker & payer): County / Municipal CDC and Health Commissions
  • End user (operator): Township health center medical staff
  • Final beneficiary: Snakebite victims in remote villages within a 60 km radius

Decision criteria

  • CDC / Health Commission: cost per life saved, coverage, compliance
  • Township health centers: ease of operation, reliability, delivery speed

Market size & focus regions

China’s high-risk snakebite provinces (Yunnan, Guangxi, Guizhou, Hainan) serve ~180M rural population, with ~150,000 annual snakebite cases requiring emergency antivenom.

≈ ¥3B / year addressable emergency delivery service market (China)

Entry strategy: pilot partnerships with county health commissions and health centers in high-incidence, hard-transport regions.

Pilot roadmap

  • Phase 1 (Months 1–6): 2–3 partner health centers in Yunnan/Guangxi; 100 real emergency deliveries
  • Phase 2 (Months 7–12): expand to 10–15 centers; standardize protocols + training
  • Phase 3 (Year 2): scale to 50+ locations via government procurement; cover 5,000 villages

Competition & positioning

  • Ground ambulances: slow on rough terrain (~30 km/h), infrastructure-limited, variable cost
  • Multicopter drones: short range (<30 km), low speed (30–40 km/h), limited radius
  • Commercial fixed-wing VTOL (e.g., SF Express / JD Logistics): not purpose-built for medical cold-chain and emergency priority authorization

VitalWing’s edge: purpose-built medical cold-chain + high-reliability transition control for mountainous emergency logistics.
